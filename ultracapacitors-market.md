<p><p>Ultracapacitors, also known as supercapacitors, are energy storage devices that use double layer capacitance and Faraday capacitance for their operation. Double layer capacitance involves storing energy through the separation of charge at the interface between an electrode and an electrolyte, providing high power density. Faraday capacitance, on the other hand, involves the reversible redox reactions within the electrode material, allowing for higher energy density. These different market types cater to varying needs for high power or high energy storage applications.</p></p>
