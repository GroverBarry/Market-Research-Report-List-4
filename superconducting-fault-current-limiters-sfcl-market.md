<p><p>Superconducting Fault Current Limiters (SFCL) are classified into four different types based on their design and technology. Shielded-Core SFCL utilize a shielded core for enhanced performance. Saturable-Core SFCL use a saturable core material to limit fault currents. Hybrid Resistive SFCL combine superconducting and resistive elements for better efficiency. Purely Resistive SFCL rely solely on resistive materials to limit fault currents. Each type offers unique advantages and applications in managing fault currents in electrical grids.</p></p>
