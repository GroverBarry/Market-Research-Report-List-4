<p><p>Medical enteral feeding tubes are devices used to deliver nutrition directly into the gastrointestinal tract of patients who are unable to consume food orally. The three main types of enteral feeding tubes are gastrostomy tubes, which are inserted through the abdominal wall into the stomach; nasoenteric tubes, which are inserted through the nose into the stomach or small intestine; and other types of tubes that may be inserted through different routes depending on the patient's needs. Each type of feeding tube serves a specific purpose in providing essential nutrition to patients who cannot eat normally.</p></p>
