This includes the use of EEG (Electroencephalography) to monitor brain function, EMG (Electromyography) to monitor muscle activity, and Evoked Potential (EP) to monitor the integrity of sensory pathways. These monitoring techniques help surgeons identify any potential issues in real-time and make necessary adjustments during the surgery to minimize the risk of neurological complications. The market for these monitoring types continues to grow as the importance of preserving neurological function during surgery is recognized.</p></p>
