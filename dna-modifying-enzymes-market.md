<p><p>DNA modifying enzymes market types include Alkaline Phosphatases, which are used to dephosphorylate DNA and RNA; Exonucleases, which cleave nucleotides from the ends of DNA strands; Endonucleases, which cleave DNA at specific recognition sequences; and Terminal Transferases, which add nucleotides to the 3' end of DNA strands. These enzymes are crucial tools in molecular biology research, genetic engineering, and diagnostics, as they allow for precise manipulation of DNA sequences for various applications.</p></p>
