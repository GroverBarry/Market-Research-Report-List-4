<p><p>Touchless sensors are devices that detect and respond to physical input without direct contact. Infrared sensors use IR light to detect motion or proximity. Ultrasonic sound wave sensors emit and receive sound waves to measure distance. Electric/capacitive near field sensors detect touch or proximity using electrical fields. Other types of touchless sensors may include technologies like laser or radar. Each type of sensor has its own advantages and applications, ranging from touchless faucets in public restrooms to automatic doors in commercial buildings.</p></p>
