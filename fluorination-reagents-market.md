<p><p>Fluorination reagents are compounds used to introduce fluorine atoms into organic molecules. There are two main types of fluorination reagents: electrophilic and nucleophilic. Electrophilic fluorination reagents are electron-deficient compounds that react with nucleophiles to form carbon-fluorine bonds. Nucleophilic fluorination reagents, on the other hand, are electron-rich compounds that react with electrophiles to form carbon-fluorine bonds. Both types of reagents play a crucial role in the synthesis of fluorinated compounds in the pharmaceutical, agrochemical, and materials industries.</p></p>
