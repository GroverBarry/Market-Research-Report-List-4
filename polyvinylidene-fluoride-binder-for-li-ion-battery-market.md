<p><ul><li>Suspension Polymerization</li><li>Emulsion Polymerization</li></ul></p>
<p><p>Polyvinylidene fluoride (PVDF) binders for Li-ion batteries are commonly produced through two main methods: suspension polymerization and emulsion polymerization. In suspension polymerization, PVDF is formed into particles suspended in a liquid medium, resulting in a binder with high purity and stability. Emulsion polymerization involves creating an emulsion of PVDF monomers in water, leading to a binder with better dispersibility and rheological properties. Both methods are widely used in the market to create PVDF binders suitable for various applications in Li-ion batteries.</p></p>
