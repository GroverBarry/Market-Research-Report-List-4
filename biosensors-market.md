<p><p>Biosensors are classified into non-wearable and wearable categories based on their design and application. Non-wearable biosensors are typically used in laboratory settings or integrated into medical devices for continuous monitoring of specific biomarkers. On the other hand, wearable biosensors are portable devices that can be worn on the body to track various health parameters in real-time. Both types of biosensors play a crucial role in healthcare, biotechnology, and environmental monitoring by providing quick and accurate data for analysis and decision-making.</p></p>
