<p><p>Single-phase power transformers are classified into three main markets based on voltage levels: low-voltage, medium-voltage, and high-voltage. Low-voltage transformers are designed for applications where the input and output voltages are below 1000V, such as in residential and commercial settings. Medium-voltage transformers are used in industrial applications where voltages range from 1000V to 72.5kV. High-voltage transformers are designed for power transmission and distribution systems, with voltages typically exceeding 72.5kV. Each market type caters to specific voltage requirements and applications.</p></p>
